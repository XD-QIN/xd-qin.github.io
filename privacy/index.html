Privacy Policy

This is a personal photography and writing site. It is built to respect your privacy by default: no cookies, no advertising, no cross-site tracking, no accounts, and no forms that collect your personal data. This page explains the little data that is involved and the rights you have, wherever you live.

Last updated: 1 January 2026.

Who is responsible

This site is operated by me, Your Name. For the purposes of data-protection law I am the “data controller” (EU/UK GDPR), the individual accountable for personal information (Canada’s PIPEDA), and the person in charge of personal information (Québec’s Law 25). You can reach me through the contact page.

The short version

  • No cookies are set by this site.
  • No advertising, ad networks, or cross-site tracking.
  • No accounts, logins, or newsletters.
  • No contact forms — nothing on this site asks you to type in personal data.
  • No analytics and no visitor tracking of any kind — nothing is stored on your device except an optional theme preference.
  • I do not sell, rent, or trade your information, and I never share it for marketing.

What is collected, and why

Analytics

This site uses no analytics — no third-party analytics service, no first-party visitor counter, and no tracking script of any kind. Nothing is loaded or stored to measure your visit, and you are not tracked across sites or over time.

Server logs (hosting provider)

The site is served by a third-party hosting provider. As with essentially every website, the host automatically records standard technical request data — including your IP address, the page requested, and your browser type — to deliver the site reliably and protect it against abuse and attacks. These logs are retained for a short period under the provider’s own policies.

Your theme preference

If you switch between light and dark mode, that single choice is saved in your browser’s local storage so the site remembers it on your next visit. It stays on your device, is never sent to me, and is not used to identify you. You can clear it at any time through your browser settings.

Links to other sites

The site links out to profiles such as Instagram, LinkedIn, and GitHub. Once you follow one of those links you are on a third-party service governed by its own privacy policy, not this one.

Cookies and similar technologies

This site uses no cookies. The only thing stored on your device is the optional theme preference described above, which is strictly necessary to remember your display choice. Because nothing non-essential is stored on or read from your device, no cookie-consent banner is required under the EU ePrivacy rules or the UK’s PECR. I also honour “Do Not Track” and Global Privacy Control browser signals — though in practice there is nothing to opt out of, because the site does not track you or build a profile of you.

Legal bases (EU/UK GDPR)

Where the GDPR or UK GDPR applies, the limited processing above rests on my legitimate interests (Article 6(1)(f)): keeping the site secure, available, and free of abuse. This processing is minimal and non-intrusive, no information identifying you is stored, and so no separate consent is required. I do not process any special-category (sensitive) data, and there is no automated decision-making or profiling.

Who I share information with

I do not sell, rent, or trade personal information, and I do not share it for advertising. My hosting provider processes limited technical data (such as request logs) on my behalf, strictly to serve and protect the site. They act as a processor under my instructions. I may disclose information only if I am legally required to do so.

International data transfers

This site is reachable worldwide, and the providers above may process data in countries other than your own, including the United States. Where required for visitors in the EU, the UK, or Canada, those transfers rely on appropriate safeguards — such as the providers’ Standard Contractual Clauses and data-processing terms.

How long data is kept

I keep no personal data about you myself. My hosting provider’s technical logs are retained for the short periods set out in its own policy.

Your rights

Your rights depend on where you live. Because this site does not store information that identifies you, I often will not be able to connect a request to any specific data about you — but you are welcome to exercise any of the rights below.

European Union & United Kingdom

Under the GDPR and UK GDPR you have the right to access, rectify, erase, restrict, or object to the processing of your personal data (including objecting to legitimate-interest processing), and the right to data portability. You will not be subject to automated decision-making. You may also lodge a complaint with your local supervisory authority — in the UK, the Information Commissioner’s Office (ico.org.uk).

United States (including California)

Where US state privacy laws such as California’s CCPA/CPRA apply, you have the right to know, access, delete, and correct personal information, and to opt out of its sale or sharing. I do not sell or share personal information, have not done so, and do not use it for targeted advertising. I will never discriminate against you for exercising a privacy right.

Canada

Under PIPEDA and Québec’s Law 25 you have the right to access the personal information held about you, to request corrections, and to withdraw consent. You may complain to the Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada (priv.gc.ca) or, in Québec, to the Commission d’accès à l’information.

To exercise any of these rights, get in touch through the contact page. I will respond within the time required by the law that applies to you.

Children’s privacy

This site is not directed to children and I do not knowingly collect personal information from children (under 13 in the United States, or the equivalent age — up to 16 — in parts of the EU). If you believe a child has provided personal information, please contact me and I will address it.

Security

The site is fully static and served only over HTTPS, with HTTP Strict Transport Security, a strict Content-Security-Policy, and standard security headers. There is no database of personal information to be breached.

Changes to this policy

I may update this policy from time to time. Any material change will be reflected in the “Last updated” date above. Continued use of the site after a change means you accept the revised policy.

Contact

Questions about privacy, or a request about your data? Please reach me through the contact page.
